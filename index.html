Constantin Brancusi was a Romanian sculptor, painter and photographer, called the patriarch of modern sculpture and considered a pioneer of modernism.The materials Brancusi used was primarily marble, stone, bronze, wood, and metal being a pioneer in direct carving.

  • 1876- Born February 19, Hobița, Romania.
  • 1887- leave home permanently after several attempts as he had a difficult childhood, in part due to challenging relationships with his father and his brothers.
  • 1898- graduate with honors at School of Arts and Crafts from Craiova and enrole at School of Fine Arts from Bucharest.
  • 1904- moved to Paris.
  • 1905-1907- studied at École des Beaux-Arts in Paris.
    • Notable work
  • 1907-1910- The Kiss:- a sculpture carved in stone representing two figures embracing each other and they fit into a suggested cube.
  • 1910- Sleeping Muse:- it is an oval-shaped disembodied head cast in bronze.
  • 1911- Prometheus: -sculpture carved in white marble.
  • 1913- Mademoiselle Pogany: -sculpture is a portrait of Margit Pogany, a Hungarian lady made it bronze with black patina on limestone base.
  • 1915- The Newborn:- it could be a baby, an embryo, or a seed,symbolize beginning.
  • 1917-1922- Torso of a Young Man:- it's represent a male torso as a cylinder mounted on vestigial cylindrical legs, cut off at mid-thigh.
  • 1919- Bird in Space:- polished-bronze symbolize the flight.
  • 1937-The Silence Table:- is a circular stone table surrounded by twelve hourglass-seats, which symbolize time.
  • 1938- The Endless Column:- sculpture is a memorial to Romanian soldiers who fought in World War I, it's standing 29.3 meters tall and it's represents the infinite connection between heaven and earth.
  • 1938-1957- as he become so faimous he travel over the world participating at exibitions and in same time he continous his work with many sculptures.
  • 1957- Died March 16,Paris, France.

His artwork can be found in different museums and have high value.In 2009 Madame L.R. has been sold for $37.2 million, setting a record price for a sculpture sold at auction.
